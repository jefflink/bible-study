### ✦ SECTION 1 — Paul's Salutation (Verses 1–4)

> *"Paul, a servant of God, and an apostle of Jesus Christ, according to the faith of God's elect, and the acknowledging of the truth which is after godliness; In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began; But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour; To Titus, mine own son after the common faith: Grace, mercy, and peace, from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Saviour."*
> — **Titus 1:1–4 (KJV)**

| Verse | Key Point |
|---|---|
| **v.1a** | Paul calls himself **"a servant of God"** — unique title; usually he says servant of Christ |
| **v.1b** | His apostleship serves **the faith of God's elect** — a mission for the chosen of God |
| **v.1c** | Truth is inseparably linked to **godliness** — true doctrine always produces holy living |
| **v.2** | Eternal life was **promised before the world began** — God's plan of salvation is eternal, not an afterthought |
| **v.2b** | **"God, that cannot lie"** — His promise is absolutely certain (see Numbers 23:19; Hebrews 6:18) |
| **v.3** | The Word is revealed **"in due times"** — God's perfect timing in unfolding His plan |
| **v.4** | Titus is called **"mine own son after the common faith"** — a spiritual father/son relationship |
| **v.4b** | Paul's greeting includes **Grace, mercy, and peace** — note mercy is added here, unlike most of his other letters |

### ✦ SECTION 2 — Qualifications of Elders (Verses 5–9)

> *"For this cause left I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting, and ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee."*
> — **Titus 1:5 (KJV)**

**Why Crete?**
Crete was a large island known for moral corruption and disorder. Paul had left Titus there specifically to **organize the churches** and appoint qualified leadership.

> — **Titus 1:7 (KJV)**

**Note:** The word **"bishop"** (v.7) and **"elder"** (v.5) are used interchangeably here — referring to the same office of church overseer.

### ✦ SECTION 3 — Warning Against False Teachers (Verses 10–16)

> *"For there are many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the circumcision: Whose mouths must be stopped, who subvert whole houses, teaching things which they ought not, for filthy lucre's sake."*
> — **Titus 1:10–11 (KJV)**

| Verse | Key Point |
|---|---|
| **v.10** | **"They of the circumcision"** — Jewish legalists insisting on circumcision for salvation (see Acts 15:1) |
| **v.10** | Three descriptions: **unruly** (insubordinate), **vain talkers** (empty speech), **deceivers** |
| **v.11** | Their mouths must be **stopped** — false teaching must be confronted, not ignored |
| **v.11** | They **"subvert whole houses"** — false doctrine destroys families and entire congregations |
| **v.11** | Their motive: **filthy lucre** — teaching for financial gain |
| **v.12** | Paul quotes a Cretan prophet (Epimenides, c. 600 BC): *"The Cretians are alway liars, evil beasts, slow bellies"* |
| **v.13** | Paul says: **"This witness is true"** — he affirms the cultural reputation of Crete's moral failure |
| **v.13b** | The solution: **rebuke them sharply** — for the goal of restoration to sound faith |
| **v.14** | Warns against **Jewish fables and commandments of men** — tradition that contradicts truth |
| **v.15** | *"Unto the pure all things are pure"* — a defiled conscience corrupts even neutral things |
| **v.16** | The greatest indictment: **"They profess that they know God; but in works they deny him"** |

## 🕊️ Key Spiritual Lessons from Titus 1

| Lesson | Scripture |
|---|---|
| **Sound leadership is essential** for a healthy church | v.5–9 |
| **Doctrine and conduct are inseparable** — truth produces godliness | v.1 |
| **False teachers must be identified and rebuked**, not tolerated | v.10–11, 13 |
| **A pure heart sees purely** — inward corruption distorts everything | v.15 |
| **Profession without practice is denial** — works reveal true faith | v.16 |
