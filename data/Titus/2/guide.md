📖 Verse-by-Verse Breakdown

✦ SECTION 1 — Instructions to Older Men (Verses 1–2)
> *“But speak thou the things which become sound doctrine: That the aged men be sober, grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience.”*
> — **Titus 2:1–2 (KJV)**
Note: Verse 1 opens with “But” — a direct contrast to the false teachers described in Chapter 1. Titus is to be the opposite of those deceivers.



|Verse  |Qualification    |Meaning                                        |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------------------------------------|
|**v.1**|Sound doctrine   |Healthy, correct teaching that shapes behaviour|
|**v.2**|Sober            |Clear-minded; not given to excess              |
|**v.2**|Grave            |Dignified, worthy of respect                   |
|**v.2**|Temperate        |Self-controlled in all areas                   |
|**v.2**|Sound in faith   |Firm and healthy in belief                     |
|**v.2**|Sound in charity |Genuine, mature love                           |
|**v.2**|Sound in patience|Steadfast endurance under trial                |

Key Insight: The older man in the church is to model maturity in three areas — Faith (toward God), Charity (toward others), and Patience (under trial). These mirror the three great virtues of 1 Corinthians 13:13 — Faith, Hope, and Love.

✦ SECTION 2 — Instructions to Older Women (Verses 3–4a)
“The aged women likewise, that they be in behaviour as becometh holiness, not false accusers, not given to much wine, teachers of good things; That they may teach the young women to be sober.”
— Titus 2:3–4a (KJV)



|Verse   |Qualification                |Meaning                                                                             |
|--------|-----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**v.3** |Behaviour becometh holiness  |Conduct that reflects a sacred, priestly life                                       |
|**v.3** |Not false accusers           |Not slanderers — *“false accusers”* is the Greek *diabolos* — same word as **devil**|
|**v.3** |Not given to much wine       |Not enslaved to alcohol                                                             |
|**v.3** |Teachers of good things      |Active in mentoring and passing on godly wisdom                                     |
|**v.4a**|Teach young women to be sober|Their primary ministry — **discipling younger women**                               |

Key Insight: The word “false accusers” in the Greek is diabolos — the same root word for devil/slanderer. A gossiping, slanderous tongue is literally doing the devil’s work.

✦ SECTION 3 — Instructions to Young Women (Verses 4b–5)
“To love their husbands, to love their children, To be discreet, chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed.”
— Titus 2:4b–5 (KJV)



|Verse  |Qualification             |Meaning                                                             |
|-------|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**v.4**|Love their husbands       |Affectionate, devoted love — *philandros* in Greek (friendship-love)|
|**v.4**|Love their children       |Tender, nurturing love for their children                           |
|**v.5**|Discreet                  |Sound-minded, sensible, self-controlled                             |
|**v.5**|Chaste                    |Pure in thought, speech, and conduct                                |
|**v.5**|Keepers at home           |Guardians of the home — managing household well                     |
|**v.5**|Good                      |Morally good; kind and benevolent                                   |
|**v.5**|Obedient to husbands      |Submissive to their own husbands (see Ephesians 5:22)               |
|**v.5**|Word of God not blasphemed|The **purpose** — godly conduct protects the reputation of Scripture|

”…that the word of God be not blasphemed.”
— Titus 2:5b (KJV)
Key Insight: The ultimate reason for godly womanhood is not cultural but theological — so that the Word of God is honoured, not dishonoured before a watching world.

✦ SECTION 4 — Instructions to Young Men (Verses 6–8)
“Young men likewise exhort to be sober minded. In all things shewing thyself a pattern of good works: in doctrine shewing uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity, Sound speech, that cannot be condemned; that he that is of the contrary part may be ashamed, having no evil thing to say of you.”
— Titus 2:6–8 (KJV)



|Verse  |Instruction              |Meaning                                                                  |
|-------|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**v.6**|Sober minded             |The one repeated command for young men — master self-control             |
|**v.7**|Pattern of good works    |Titus himself is to be a **living example** — lead by life not just words|
|**v.7**|Uncorruptness in doctrine|Teaching that is pure, uncontaminated                                    |
|**v.7**|Gravity                  |Seriousness and dignity in ministry                                      |
|**v.7**|Sincerity                |Genuine, without hypocrisy                                               |
|**v.8**|Sound speech             |Words that are healthy and wholesome                                     |
|**v.8**|Cannot be condemned      |Living so blamelessly that critics have nothing valid to say             |
|**v.8**|Contrary part ashamed    |Opponents silenced by the quality of godly living                        |

Key Insight: For young men, the primary command is “sober minded” — appearing once here (v.6), but the concept echoes throughout. Young men are especially prone to passion, impulse, and pride. The antidote is sober-mindedness — disciplined thinking and self-mastery.
“In all things shewing thyself a pattern of good works.”
— Titus 2:7 (KJV)
Titus himself is part of the instruction — he is not just to teach young men but to be a living model before them.

✦ SECTION 5 — Instructions to Servants (Verses 9–10)
“Exhort servants to be obedient unto their own masters, and to please them well in all things; not answering again; Not purloining, but shewing all good fidelity; that they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things.”
— Titus 2:9–10 (KJV)



|Verse   |Instruction                   |Meaning                                                   |
|--------|------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
|**v.9** |Obedient to masters           |Submit to authority in the workplace                      |
|**v.9** |Please them well in all things|Work diligently and excellently                           |
|**v.9** |Not answering again           |No back-talk or insubordination                           |
|**v.10**|Not purloining                |Not stealing — not pilfering from employers               |
|**v.10**|Shewing all good fidelity     |Completely trustworthy and loyal                          |
|**v.10**|Adorn the doctrine of God     |Their conduct **beautifies** the Gospel before unbelievers|

”…that they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things.”
— Titus 2:10b (KJV)
Key Insight: The word “adorn” (kosmeo in Greek) means to decorate or beautify — the same root as our word cosmetics. A servant’s faithful conduct makes the Gospel attractive and beautiful to the watching world. Every Christian’s daily work is a display of the doctrine they profess.

✦ SECTION 6 — The Grace of God (Verses 11–14) ⭐ Heart of the Chapter
“For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men, Teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world; Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ; Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works.”
— Titus 2:11–14 (KJV)

📊 The Three Tenses of Salvation in Titus 2:11–13



|Tense      |Verse|Description                                                |Theological Term                             |
|-----------|:---:|-----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
|**Past**   |v.11 |*“Grace…hath appeared”* — The First Coming of Christ       |**Justification** — Saved from sin’s penalty |
|**Present**|v.12 |*“We should live soberly, righteously, godly”* — Living now|**Sanctification** — Saved from sin’s power  |
|**Future** |v.13 |*“Looking for that blessed hope”* — The Second Coming      |**Glorification** — Saved from sin’s presence|

📖 Breaking Down Verses 11–14



|Verse    |Key Point                                                                                                   |
|---------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**v.11** |Grace **“appeared”** — the incarnation of Christ is grace made visible to all mankind                       |
|**v.12a**|Grace **teaches** us — salvation is not just pardon, it is a school of holy living                          |
|**v.12b**|**Denying ungodliness** — actively rejecting what dishonours God                                            |
|**v.12c**|**Three dimensions of godly living**: Soberly (toward self), Righteously (toward others), Godly (toward God)|
|**v.13** |**“The blessed hope”** — the Second Coming of Christ; a purifying hope (see 1 John 3:3)                     |
|**v.13b**|*“The great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ”* — a clear declaration of **Christ’s full deity**             |
|**v.14a**|*“Who gave himself for us”* — voluntary substitutionary atonement                                           |
|**v.14b**|*“Redeem us from all iniquity”* — purchased freedom from the bondage of sin                                 |
|**v.14c**|*“A peculiar people”* — not strange, but a **purchased, special possession** (see Exodus 19:5; 1 Peter 2:9) |
|**v.14d**|*“Zealous of good works”* — the redeemed are not passive; they are **eagerly active** in good works         |

🔍 Three Dimensions of Godly Living — Titus 2:12



|Dimension      |Direction        |Meaning                                |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------------------------------|
|**Soberly**    |Toward **Self**  |Disciplined, self-controlled inner life|
|**Righteously**|Toward **Others**|Just and upright in all relationships  |
|**Godly**      |Toward **God**   |Devoted, reverent, and holy before God |

✦ SECTION 7 — Titus’s Charge (Verse 15)
“These things speak, and exhort, and rebuke with all authority. Let no man despise thee.”
— Titus 2:15 (KJV)



|Action                     |Meaning                                             |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
|**Speak**                  |Declare these truths plainly and clearly            |
|**Exhort**                 |Urge and encourage people to apply them             |
|**Rebuke**                 |Correct those who deviate — with full authority     |
|**Let no man despise thee**|Do not allow your youth or position to be undermined|

Key Insight: Titus is given three tools of ministry — speaking, exhorting, and rebuking. All three are necessary. A minister who only speaks but never rebukes allows error to grow. One who only rebukes but never exhorts discourages the flock.
