## 📖 Verse-by-Verse Breakdown

### ✦ SECTION 1 — Civil Duties & Gentle Conduct (Verses 1–2)

> *"Put them in mind to be subject to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates, to be ready to every good work, To speak evil of no man, to be no brawlers, but gentle, shewing all meekness unto all men."*
> — **Titus 3:1–2 (KJV)**

**Note:** After addressing behaviour within the church (Chapter 2), Paul now turns to the **Christian's conduct in society and toward all people.**

---

#### 📊 Christian Duties in Society — Titus 3:1–2

| Verse | Duty | Direction | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **v.1** | Subject to principalities & powers | Toward **Government** | Submit to ruling authorities — civil government |
| **v.1** | Obey magistrates | Toward **Government** | Comply with civil laws and officials |
| **v.1** | Ready to every good work | Toward **Society** | Always prepared and eager to do what is right |
| **v.2** | Speak evil of no man | Toward **All People** | No slander or defamation of anyone |
| **v.2** | No brawlers | Toward **All People** | Not contentious or quarrelsome |
| **v.2** | Gentle | Toward **All People** | Considerate, kind, yielding in spirit |
| **v.2** | Meekness unto all men | Toward **All People** | Humble in attitude toward every person |

> **Key Insight:** The phrase **"all men"** is significant — this meekness and gentleness is not reserved for fellow believers only. Christians are to display this conduct **toward every human being**, including enemies, unbelievers, and hostile authorities.

---

#### 🔗 Cross-Reference — Submission to Authority

| Scripture | Connection |
|---|---|
| **Romans 13:1–2** | *"Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers...the powers that be are ordained of God"* |
| **1 Peter 2:13–14** | *"Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord's sake"* |
| **1 Timothy 2:1–2** | Pray for kings and all in authority |

### ✦ SECTION 2 — Before & After Salvation (Verses 3–7) ⭐ *Heart of the Chapter*

This section is one of the most **doctrinally rich** passages in the entire letter. Paul contrasts what believers **were** before salvation with what **God did** for them in Christ.

---

#### 📊 What We WERE — Titus 3:3

> *"For we ourselves also were sometimes foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving divers lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, and hating one another."*
> — **Titus 3:3 (KJV)**

| Description | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Foolish** | Without spiritual understanding; morally senseless |
| **Disobedient** | Refusing to submit to God's authority |
| **Deceived** | Led astray; living under the deception of sin and Satan |
| **Serving divers lusts and pleasures** | Enslaved to various sinful desires and self-gratification |
| **Living in malice** | Habitual ill-will and wickedness toward others |
| **Envy** | Resentment of others' blessings and success |
| **Hateful** | Detestable in character and conduct |
| **Hating one another** | Active hatred expressed in relationships |

> **Key Insight:** Paul uses **"we"** — he includes himself, Titus, and all believers. This is not a description of pagans only. It is the **universal condition of mankind apart from God's grace**, regardless of background or religion. This humility is the foundation for treating others (v.2) with meekness — remembering what we once were.

---

#### 📊 What GOD DID — Titus 3:4–7

> *"But after that the kindness and love of God our Saviour toward man appeared, Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy Ghost; Which he shed on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Saviour; That being justified by his grace, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life."*
> — **Titus 3:4–7 (KJV)**

---

#### 📊 The Five Great Acts of God in Salvation — Titus 3:4–7

| Act | Verse | Description |
|---|:---:|---|
| **Kindness & Love Appeared** | v.4 | God's grace was manifested in the incarnation of Christ |
| **He Saved Us** | v.5 | Salvation is entirely God's action — not human merit |
| **Washing of Regeneration** | v.5 | The new birth — a complete spiritual cleansing and new beginning |
| **Renewing of the Holy Ghost** | v.5 | The ongoing transforming work of the Holy Spirit within the believer |
| **Justified by His Grace** | v.7 | Declared righteous before God — not by works but by grace |

---

#### 🔍 Verse-by-Verse Detail — Titus 3:4–7

| Verse | Key Point |
|---|---|
| **v.4** | **"Kindness and love of God appeared"** — the word for love here is *philanthropia* — God's love for **mankind**; this is the incarnation of Christ |
| **v.5a** | **"Not by works of righteousness which we have done"** — salvation is absolutely **not earned** by human effort or merit |
| **v.5b** | **"According to his mercy"** — the sole ground of salvation is **God's mercy** — His compassion toward the undeserving |
| **v.5c** | **"Washing of regeneration"** — *paliggenesia* — rebirth; a complete spiritual cleansing (see John 3:3–5; Ezekiel 36:25–26) |
| **v.5d** | **"Renewing of the Holy Ghost"** — the Spirit's continual work of transformation within the believer (see Romans 12:2) |
| **v.6** | **"Shed on us abundantly"** — the Holy Spirit is not given sparingly — He is poured out richly through Christ |
| **v.7a** | **"Justified by his grace"** — declared righteous — not because of what we did but what Christ did |
| **v.7b** | **"Heirs according to the hope of eternal life"** — salvation results in **inheritance** — we are God's children and heirs (see Romans 8:17) |

---

#### 📊 Contrast: What We Were vs. What God Did

| Our Condition (v.3) | God's Action (v.4–7) |
|---|---|
| Foolish | He gave us wisdom through His Word |
| Disobedient | He gave us new hearts to obey |
| Deceived | He revealed truth through Christ |
| Slaves to lusts | He washed and renewed us |
| Living in malice | He shed His love in our hearts |
| Hateful & hating | He made us heirs of eternal life |

---

#### 🔍 The Trinity in Salvation — Titus 3:4–6

| Person | Role | Verse |
|---|---|---|
| **God the Father** | The Source — His kindness, mercy, and grace initiated salvation | v.4–5, 7 |
| **God the Son (Jesus Christ)** | The Channel — shed the Holy Spirit abundantly through Him | v.6 |
| **God the Holy Spirit** | The Agent — washing of regeneration and renewing | v.5 |

> **Key Insight:** The entire **Holy Trinity is active in the salvation of a soul** — planned by the Father, purchased by the Son, and applied by the Holy Spirit.

### ✦ SECTION 3 — The Faithful Saying (Verse 8)

> *"This is a faithful saying, and these things I will that thou affirm constantly, that they which have believed in God might be careful to maintain good works. These things are good and profitable unto men."*
> — **Titus 3:8 (KJV)**

| Element | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **"Faithful saying"** | A confirmed, trustworthy doctrinal statement — Paul uses this phrase 5 times in the Pastoral Epistles |
| **"Affirm constantly"** | Titus is to continually and firmly insist on these truths |
| **"Believed in God"** | Those who have placed their trust in God through Christ |
| **"Careful to maintain good works"** | The saved are to be **diligent and intentional** about good works |
| **"Good and profitable unto men"** | Good works benefit not just the doer but the whole of society |

> **Key Insight:** Good works do **not produce** salvation — but salvation **always produces** good works. The saved person who has experienced God's mercy (v.3–7) is motivated and equipped to live differently (v.8). This is the direct logical connection Paul makes.

---

### ✦ SECTION 4 — Avoid Foolish Controversies (Verses 9–11)

> *"But avoid foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions, and strivings about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain. A man that is an heretick after the first and second admonition reject; Knowing that he that is such is subverted, and sinneth, being condemned of himself."*
> — **Titus 3:9–11 (KJV)**

---

#### 📊 What to AVOID — Titus 3:9

| What to Avoid | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Foolish questions** | Pointless, speculative debates that produce no spiritual fruit |
| **Genealogies** | Endless debates over Jewish family lineages used to establish superiority or false doctrine |
| **Contentions** | Quarrels and disputes that divide rather than edify |
| **Strivings about the law** | Arguments over legal requirements for salvation — the Judaizer problem (see Chapter 1:10) |

> *"For they are unprofitable and vain."* — **Titus 3:9b (KJV)**

> **Key Insight:** Paul does not say these debates are merely unpleasant — he declares them **unprofitable and vain** — producing zero spiritual benefit. A church that constantly debates secondary matters neglects the primary calling of proclaiming the Gospel and maintaining good works.

---

#### 📊 How to Handle a Heretic — Titus 3:10–11

| Step | Verse | Action |
|---|:---:|---|
| **First Admonition** | v.10 | Warn the divisive person — clearly and biblically |
| **Second Admonition** | v.10 | Warn again — give a second opportunity to repent |
| **Reject** | v.10 | After two warnings, **withdraw from** and **refuse further engagement** with him |

| Why Reject? | Verse | Reason |
|---|:---:|---|
| **He is subverted** | v.11 | His thinking is twisted and warped from the truth |
| **He sinneth** | v.11 | His divisiveness is active, ongoing sin |
| **Condemned of himself** | v.11 | His own persistent rejection of correction pronounces judgment on himself |

> **Key Insight:** The word **"heretick"** (*hairetikos*) means one who **causes divisions** by choosing his own opinions over sound doctrine. The two-warning process reflects both **grace** (giving opportunity to repent) and **firmness** (refusing to let division destroy the church). This mirrors the pattern in Matthew 18:15–17.

---

### ✦ SECTION 5 — Final Instructions (Verses 12–14)

> *"When I shall send Artemas unto thee, or Tychicus, be diligent to come unto me to Nicopolis: for I have determined there to winter. Bring Zenas the lawyer and Apollos on their journey diligently, that nothing be wanting unto them. And let ours also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful."*
> — **Titus 3:12–14 (KJV)**

| Verse | Instruction | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **v.12** | Artemas or Tychicus to be sent | Paul plans to send a replacement so Titus can join him |
| **v.12** | Come to Nicopolis | Paul's winter location — likely Nicopolis in Epirus, Greece |
| **v.13** | Help Zenas & Apollos on their journey | Provide generously for travelling ministers — hospitality in action |
| **v.13** | That nothing be wanting | Ensure they have everything they need for their journey |
| **v.14** | Maintain good works for necessary uses | Practical application — good works meeting real, tangible needs |
| **v.14** | That they be not unfruitful | Believers who do not maintain good works are **unfruitful** — a serious warning |

---

#### 📊 People Mentioned in Titus 3:12–13

| Person | Who They Were |
|---|---|
| **Artemas** | Companion of Paul — mentioned only here in Scripture |
| **Tychicus** | Faithful minister; carried Paul's letters (see Ephesians 6:21; Colossians 4:7) |
| **Zenas the lawyer** | Believed to be a former Jewish scribe or Roman lawyer; only mentioned here |
| **Apollos** | Gifted preacher from Alexandria (see Acts 18:24–28; 1 Corinthians 3:6) |

---

### ✦ SECTION 6 — Closing Benediction (Verse 15)

> *"All that are with me salute thee. Greet them that love us in the faith. Grace be with you all. Amen."*
> — **Titus 3:15 (KJV)**

| Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **"All that are with me salute thee"** | Paul's companions send their greetings to Titus |
| **"Them that love us in the faith"** | The bond of Christian love defined by shared faith in Christ |
| **"Grace be with you all"** | Paul's characteristic closing — grace is both the beginning (1:4) and the end of the letter |
| **"Amen"** | So be it — a solemn affirmation |

> **Key Insight:** Paul **opens and closes** with grace — *"Grace, mercy, and peace"* (1:4) and *"Grace be with you all"* (3:15). The entire letter is **framed by grace** — the undeserved favour of God that is the foundation of everything Paul teaches.

## 📊 Complete Overview — The Flow of Titus 3

| Movement | Verses | Truth |
|---|:---:|---|
| **Our Duty to Society** | 1–2 | Christians submit, obey, and show meekness to all |
| **Why Meekness?** | 3 | Remember what we once were — no room for pride |
| **What Changed Us?** | 4–7 | God's mercy, grace, and the work of the Trinity |
| **What Should We Do Now?** | 8 | Maintain good works diligently |
| **What Should We Avoid?** | 9–11 | Foolish debates and divisive people |
| **Practical Closing** | 12–15 | Serve, support, and show good works in real life |

## 🕊️ Key Spiritual Lessons from Titus 3

| Lesson | Verses |
|---|---|
| **Remembering our past humbles us** — we were all once lost, making us gentle toward others | v.3 |
| **Salvation is entirely of God** — His kindness, mercy, and grace; nothing of our own works | v.4–5 |
| **All three Persons of the Trinity work together** in saving a soul | v.4–6 |
| **Doctrine must produce action** — the saved are to be zealous and careful about good works | v.8 |
| **Not all religious debate is profitable** — some arguments must simply be avoided | v.9 |
| **Church discipline is both gracious and firm** — warn twice, then withdraw | v.10–11 |
| **Grace frames everything** — it is the beginning and end of the Christian life | v.1, 15 |
