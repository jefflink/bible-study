## 🗺️ Historical Background

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **The Divided Kingdom** | After Solomon, Israel split into two kingdoms — **Northern Israel** (10 tribes) and **Southern Judah** (2 tribes) in c. 930 BC |
| **Northern Israel's Condition** | Deeply corrupt — idolatry, Baal worship, social injustice, political instability |
| **Jeroboam II** | The king of Israel during much of Hosea's ministry — a time of material prosperity but spiritual bankruptcy |
| **The Assyrian Threat** | Assyria was rising as the dominant world power and would eventually **conquer and exile Northern Israel in 722 BC** |
| **Contemporary Prophets** | Isaiah, Amos, and Micah were prophesying at the same time |

## 👤 Who Was Hosea?

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| **Father** | Beeri (Hosea 1:1) |
| **Wife** | Gomer, daughter of Diblaim |
| **Children** | Jezreel, Lo-ruhamah, Lo-ammi |
| **Occupation** | Prophet — his entire life became a **living parable** of God's message |
| **Unique Calling** | Only prophet commanded by God to **marry an unfaithful woman** as an object lesson |
| **Character** | A man of deep feeling — his personal heartbreak mirrors God's heartbreak over Israel |

---

## 📜 The Central Message of Hosea

> Hosea's marriage to **Gomer** — who was unfaithful and eventually left him — is a **living parable** of Israel's unfaithfulness to God. Just as Gomer played the harlot against Hosea, Israel committed spiritual harlotry against the LORD by chasing after false gods (Baal worship). Yet just as Hosea **went after Gomer and restored her**, God declares He will **restore and redeem Israel** in His unfailing love.

## 📜 Structure of the Book of Hosea

| Section | Chapters | Title |
|---|:---:|---|
