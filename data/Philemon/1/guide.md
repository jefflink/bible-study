---

## 📖 Verse-by-Verse Breakdown

---

### ✦ SECTION 1 — Greeting (Verses 1–3)

> *"Paul, a prisoner of Jesus Christ, and Timothy our brother, unto Philemon our dearly beloved, and fellowlabourer, And to our beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house: Grace to you, and peace, from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ."*
> — **Philemon 1:1–3 (KJV)**

**Key Observations:**
- Paul identifies himself as **"a prisoner of Jesus Christ"** — not ashamed of his chains, but anchored in Christ
- **Apphia** is believed to be Philemon's wife
- **Archippus** is believed to be his son (see Colossians 4:17)
- The **church met in Philemon's house** — a common early Christian practice
- Paul opens with his signature greeting: **Grace and Peace**

---

### ✦ SECTION 2 — Thanksgiving & Prayer (Verses 4–7)

> *"I thank my God, making mention of thee always in my prayers, Hearing of thy love and faith, which thou hast toward the Lord Jesus, and toward all saints; That the communication of thy faith may become effectual by the acknowledging of every good thing which is in you in Christ Jesus. For we have great joy and consolation in thy love, because the bowels of the saints are refreshed by thee, brother."*
> — **Philemon 1:4–7 (KJV)**

**Key Observations:**
- Paul **prays for Philemon consistently** — setting a model of intercessory prayer
- Philemon was known for his **love and faith** toward both Christ and fellow believers
- *"Bowels"* in KJV language means the **innermost affections** — the deep seat of emotion
- Paul is **strategically building up Philemon** before making his appeal — noting his character of refreshing others

---

### ✦ SECTION 3 — The Appeal for Onesimus (Verses 8–16)

> *"Wherefore, though I might be much bold in Christ to enjoin thee that which is convenient, Yet for love's sake I rather beseech thee, being such an one as Paul the aged, and now also a prisoner of Jesus Christ. I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds."*
> — **Philemon 1:8–10 (KJV)**

| Verse | Key Point |
|---|---|
| **v.8–9** | Paul has authority to command but chooses to **appeal in love** instead |
| **v.10** | Paul calls Onesimus **"my son"** — spiritually born again under Paul's ministry in prison |
| **v.11** | Onesimus means **"profitable/useful"** — Paul makes a wordplay: once unprofitable, now truly profitable |
| **v.12** | Paul sends Onesimus back — calling him **"mine own bowels"** (his own heart) |
| **v.13–14** | Paul wished to keep Onesimus to help him in prison, but would **not act without Philemon's consent** |
| **v.15–16** | God's providence — Onesimus was separated for a season so Philemon might receive him **forever, not as a slave, but as a beloved brother** |

> *"Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?"*
> — **Philemon 1:16 (KJV)** ⭐ *Key Verse*

---

### ✦ SECTION 4 — Paul's Personal Pledge (Verses 17–21)

> *"If thou count me therefore a partner, receive him as myself. If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account; I Paul have written it with mine own hand, I will repay it."*
> — **Philemon 1:17–19 (KJV)**

**Key Observations:**
- Paul says **"receive him as myself"** — a picture of substitution and advocacy
- Paul **personally guarantees any debt** Onesimus owes — he picks up the debt of another. This is a beautiful **type of Christ**, who paid our debt before God
- Paul then gently reminds Philemon: *"thou owest unto me even thine own self"* — Philemon himself came to faith through Paul
- Verse 21 — Paul expresses **confidence that Philemon will do even more** than asked

---

### ✦ SECTION 5 — Closing (Verses 22–25)

> *"But withal prepare me also a lodging: for I trust that through your prayers I shall be given unto you. There salute thee Epaphras, my fellowprisoner in Christ Jesus; Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas, my fellowlabourers. The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit. Amen."*
> — **Philemon 1:22–25 (KJV)**

- Paul expresses hope to **visit Philemon in person** soon
- He sends greetings from **5 companions**: Epaphras, Marcus (Mark), Aristarchus, Demas, and Lucas (Luke)
- Closes with a **grace benediction** — his standard letter ending

## 🕊️ Key Themes

| Theme | Explanation |
|---|---|
| **Forgiveness** | Philemon is called to forgive a wrong done to him — mirroring God's forgiveness of us |
| **Reconciliation** | A broken relationship is to be fully restored in Christ |
| **Christian Brotherhood** | Onesimus goes from slave to **"beloved brother"** — Christ levels all human distinctions |
| **Intercession** | Paul stands in the gap for Onesimus — a type of Christ standing before the Father for us |
| **Grace** | Paul does not demand — he appeals through grace and love |

---

## 🔍 Christ Typology in Philemon

One of the most beautiful aspects of this short letter is how it **pictures the Gospel:**

| Philemon's Story | Gospel Parallel |
|---|---|
| Onesimus — the runaway slave | Mankind — sinners who have fled from God |
| Paul — the intercessor | Christ — our Advocate before the Father |
| *"Receive him as myself"* (v.17) | God receives us in Christ's righteousness |
| *"Put that on mine account"* (v.18) | Christ bears our sin and debt |
| Onesimus returning to Philemon | The sinner reconciled to God |
